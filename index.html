Welcome all who love or are interested in different kinds of Ariums!

Arium is a Latin suffix meaning "container". However, the prefix tells us the difference between the many types of Ariums. For example, terra means "earth", or aqua means "water".

Photo by Katarzyna Modrzejewska

Some types of Arium

Open Terrarium

A terrarium is designed mainly to raise plants. This example is an open one and suits plants that need less water like succulents.

Photo by Jeff Sheldon on Unsplash

Sealed Terrarium

A sealed terrarium regulates water on its own; therefore, the container is always damp and suits organisms like moss and fern.

Photo by Keszthelyi Timi on Unsplash

Aquarium

An aquarium usually contains aquatic organisms.

Photo by Huy Phan on Unsplash

Vivarium

A vivarium is primarily designed to be a habitat for one or many animals.

Photo by George Millson on Unsplash
